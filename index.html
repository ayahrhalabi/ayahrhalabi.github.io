Dynamics of Air Pollution in Lebanon and It’s Impacts

Ayah Halabi, Mentor: Dr. Rameesh Singh

Abstract
Atmospheric Pollution is caused by the emissions of different sources of greenhouse gases on the earth. These emissions are tiny particles in the atmosphere, known as “aerosols,” of different sizes and volume concentrations. The nature of aerosols varies from season to season and are very dynamic and impact human health and climate. Atmospheric Pollution was analyzed over a country in the Middle East known as Lebanon. Lebanon is a Middle Eastern country located 34° N, 35°. It is surrounded by Mediterranean Sea from the west, Syria from the North, and Palestine from the South. The surface area of Lebanon is 10,400 km². The population in Lebanon during 2020 is 6,825,442 people. We considered two different regions, North and South of Lebanon with each having the perimeter of approximately 350km. Detailed analysis of satellite data have been carried out to study different types of aerosols and their sources, and their impacts on public health, agriculture, and coastal areas. This study targets long periods 2003-2020 variability over Lebanon, a small country in the Middle East. Our aim was to examine which part of Lebanon was more polluted by comparing the different greenhouse gases (carbon monoxide, methane, ozone) and atmospheric parameters (aerosol optical depth - AOD, angstrom exponent - AE). Further, we have analyzed sea temperature and chlorophyll concentrations data available through NASA Giovanni portal to study impacts of airmass along the coast reaching over Lebanon. Long term variability and trends of atmospheric parameters and greenhouse gases and their impacts will be discussed in detail.


Impacts of Mediterranean and DASH Diets on MPN Symptoms

Emi Cervantes, Monte Davityan, Ayah Halabi, Julie Truong, Molly Wu

Abstract
Myeloproliferative Neoplasms (MPN) is a cancer located within the blood and bone marrow. To address the range of symptoms that occur, 24 MPN patients' symptom scores were gathered over the course of 112 days. The Mediterranean and DASH diet were utilized for their focus on preventing cardiovascular disease, composed of whole grains, fruits, seafood, vegetables, and nuts. We argue that both diets have impact in reducing the symptoms associated with MPN. However, there is not enough evidence to warrant which diet is more influential.


WLK: Safety with Every Step

Hafsa Farooqui, Hiba Mughall, Ayah Halabi, Dahlia Sukaik

Overview
As female students, we knew how dangerous it is for us to walk on campus alone at night . Most of the times, being accomapanied by another women during that time elevates sense of safety then calling campus police who are usually male. That was the motivation behind WLK. Think about Uber but for walking; WLK allows females on campus to contact trained employees who are all female to accompany them to differnt parts of any campus. This porject was created for the Engineering Social Justice Competition at CSUF, and won 3rd place.
